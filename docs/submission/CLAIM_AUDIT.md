# Claim and limitation audit

## Use rule

Every material statement in the submission, demo, screenshots, repository story, and spoken answers must be supportable by this audit. If new evidence changes a status, record the exact candidate, environment, device/provider/account, command or observation, and date before changing the wording.

### Evidence classes

- **Implemented:** direct source/configuration evidence; existence alone does not prove runtime behavior.
- **Tested:** automated unit, contract, integration, browser, accessibility, performance, or persistence evidence on the base.
- **Locally observed:** an orchestrator-observed local runtime result, kept distinct from hosted/provider/physical evidence.
- **Official context:** fact stated by the official event or company source.
- **Pending:** requires a human/account/privacy/external gate and cannot be spoken as complete.
- **Future:** explicitly outside the prototype claim.
- **Waived:** a required check was consciously not completed, with owner/reason. No current material claim is silently waived.

## Final audit checklist

- Every product URL, repository URL, video, screenshot, device, provider, and deployment statement has evidence for the exact candidate.
- Implemented, tested, locally observed, externally pending, and future claims remain distinct.
- Test suite counts are not added into a misleading total.
- The protected production-build evidence is not used for the separate mutation-loop claim.
- WebKit/iPhone-layout evidence is not called physical iPhone/Safari.
- Provider fixtures are not called live services.
- Recorded recovery is visibly labelled and never called live capture.
- No clinical, accuracy, threshold, outcome, NHS/eMed endorsement, production-ready, real-time provider, or physical-comparison overclaim remains.
